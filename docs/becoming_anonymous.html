Zone 1 Basic Privacy

Through the internet, our personal data is sold by companies to third party marketers. Sometimes our personal information can be leaked through the internet. In this zone, we will discuss password management. A strong password is needed when online. All of your passwords can be placed into a system and be secured. Another tip is to not answer your security questions truthfully because hackers can easily find them on the web. Even phone passwords should be lengthy. Stay away from small pins and letters. Google authentication is good for privacy when you use your phone number. Also, VPN is useful because it secures your web trafficking information. Some useful tips for phone privacy are having an active VPN, disabling location, disabling Bluetooth, and don’t allow applications to your phone data.

Zone 2 more complex privacy

Now let’s talk about communication. Texting is very insecure, only iMessage and signal are good secure texting services. Another tip is changing email providers. You can forward an email to the new provider and this new provider is secure. Besides, don’t use biometrics passwords because your genetic data can be leaked. Also never used default passwords on personal routers and let the password managers create them. Stay away from Google chrome. They are known for selling personal data. Firefox is a better alternative.
